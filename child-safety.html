NūrRing Child Safety Standards (CSAE)

This page sets out the child safety standards for NūrRing (the “App”), developed and published by tomi ogunnorin. It is intended to comply with Google Play’s Child Safety Standards policy and relevant child protection laws.

1. Zero tolerance for CSAE and CSAM

NūrRing has a zero‑tolerance policy towards Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) and Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). Any content or behaviour that sexually exploits, abuses, endangers, or targets children is strictly prohibited in the App.

This includes, but is not limited to, any attempt to share, request, create, promote, or otherwise facilitate CSAM or CSAE‑related activity through NūrRing.

2. Prohibited content and behaviour

Users must not use NūrRing to engage in, promote, or facilitate any of the following:

  • Child sexual abuse or exploitation in any form, including grooming or solicitation of minors.
  • Creation, possession, distribution, or sharing of CSAM, including images, videos, text, links, or any other content that depicts or describes the sexual exploitation or abuse of children.
  • Requests for explicit or sexual content involving children, or any attempt to contact children for sexual purposes.
  • Harassment, bullying, or threats towards children, or encouraging others to harm children.
  • Any other behaviour that endangers the safety, privacy, or wellbeing of children.

NūrRing is a dhikr and wellbeing app, not a dating or explicit‑content platform. Any attempt to repurpose the app for CSAE‑related behaviour is a serious violation of these standards and of our Terms & Conditions.

3. User reporting and in‑app feedback

NūrRing provides mechanisms for users to contact support and raise concerns about content or behaviour in the App. Users who encounter anything they believe may relate to CSAE or CSAM should:

  • Use any in‑app feedback or support options to contact the developer; and/or
  • Email the dedicated child safety contact: tomiogunnorin@gmail.com.

Reports relating to CSAE, CSAM, or other serious child safety concerns are treated with the highest priority and will be reviewed and acted on as quickly as possible.

4. Handling and reporting CSAM

If we become aware of any suspected or confirmed CSAM or CSAE activity in connection with NūrRing, we will:

  • Immediately remove or disable access to the offending content.
  • Take appropriate action against the relevant account(s), which may include suspension or permanent ban.
  • Report the matter to the appropriate authorities in line with applicable law and guidance. This may include law enforcement and national or regional child protection organisations.

We will cooperate with lawful investigations and orders from competent authorities in relation to child safety and CSAE / CSAM.

5. Compliance with child safety laws

NūrRing is operated with the intention to comply with all applicable child safety and child protection laws, as well as the Google Play Developer Programme Policies on child safety and CSAE.

This includes obligations to report confirmed CSAM to relevant authorities, comply with lawful requests for information, and adjust our practices as child protection laws and platform policies evolve.

6. Age‑appropriate use and safeguards

NūrRing is designed as a dhikr and wellbeing tool for a general audience. It does not contain sexual content and is not intended for dating, adult content, or explicit interactions.

We expect all users to act respectfully, and we reserve the right to restrict or remove access where behaviour poses risks to children or violates these safety standards.

We may update these Child Safety Standards from time to time to reflect changes in law, best practice, or Google Play policies. The latest version will always be available on this page.
